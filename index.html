About Apriqot

During their decades working in public health, Ken and Kevin often faced the same challenge — making community-level decisions without access to local data. This lack of insight meant missed trends, poor resource allocation, and ineffective policies.

Early in the COVID-19 pandemic, Ken and Kevin, working from different coasts, were both trying to identify multigenerational households. Their respective agencies had the resources, but they realized most public health organizations didn't. Worse, they were using the same public datasets separately — a massive duplication of effort.

Apriqot was born to solve this problem — to make high-quality, localized, and actionable data accessible to all stakeholders, especially those with limited resources. By doing so, Apriqot empowers public health professionals to make smarter, faster, more informed decisions.

Founded in 2023, Apriqot is embedded at Northeastern University's Roux Institute through the inaugural Future of Healthcare Founders Residency.

Why Are We Called Apriqot?

There are many reasons behind our name, and yes — we like apricots! But beyond that, apricots kept popping up during our brainstorming process.

Apriqot is a metaphor: we maintain multiple versions of the places we analyze (like an orchard), each with many individuals (like apricots on trees). We call the process of integrating user data with ours grafting. And some of our estimation techniques use kernel methods — see the pattern?

There's also a color called apricot, which made design decisions easier. And with their soft blush, apricots even resemble people!

But the most "mathy" reason comes from probability theory. In public health, p represents the probability of having a condition. If q is the chance of not having the condition, then qot represents the average probability of being well in a community — a core value we stand for.

We combined "qot" with a (very boring) alternate company name idea: "Applied Population Research Inc." — and Apriqot was born!
